## What Is 6G?

- Sixth-generation wireless communications — successor to 5G
- No finalized standard yet; research and early development phase
- Expected: use higher frequencies, much higher data rates, sub-microsecond latency

**One debate:** Some argue 5G will be continuously updated (like software versions) rather than 6G being a separate standard — avoiding the huge investment of a brand-new system.

## Performance Targets (Projected)

- **Latency:** Sub-microsecond (< 1 μs) — effectively instantaneous
- **Data rate:** 100× faster than 4G LTE; 5× faster than 5G
- **Frequencies:** Will use THz (Terahertz) band — 95 GHz to 3 THz range

---

## Key Technologies Being Researched

### Terahertz (THz) Communications
- Frequencies: 0.1–10 THz (above mmWave)
- Massive bandwidth available → enormous data rates
- Challenge: Technology doesn't yet exist to make it practical
- Very high atmospheric absorption — very short range
- Korea (ETRI) targeting 6G 100× faster than 4G, 5× faster than 5G

### Next-Gen MIMO
- 6G will extend massive MIMO further — potentially hundreds or thousands of antennas
- Very short wavelengths at THz → antennas tiny → can fit many in small space

### Dense Networks
- Even smaller cells than 5G small cells
- Femtocells deployed at home/building level as standard

### New Waveforms
- OFDM has limitations (high PAPR, spectral leakage)
- Candidates: **GFDM** (Generalised FDM), **FBMC** (Filter Bank Multi-Carrier), **UFMC** (Universal Filtered Multi-Carrier)
- Each has tradeoffs; adaptive waveform switching possible

### New Modulation
- PSK and QAM have high PAPR
- **APSK** (Amplitude Phase Shift Keying) — lower PAPR → more efficient power amplifier

### Full Duplex (FD)
- Current: FDD (different frequencies) or TDD (different times)
- 6G possibility: **Same frequency, same time** for UL and DL simultaneously
- Challenge: Self-interference from own transmitter — requires advanced cancellation

### Flexible Duplex
- UL/DL ratio variable in real-time based on traffic

## What 6G Might Enable

- Communications nearly indistinguishable from instantaneous
- Full holographic communication
- True tactile internet (haptic feedback over wireless)
- Complete IoT saturation — every physical object connected
- AI-native network (AI embedded at every layer, not added on top)
