## What Is 6G?

Sixth-generation wireless communications — the successor to 5G. No finalized standard yet; the industry is in the **research and early standardization** phase. 6G is expected to use higher frequencies (THz band), achieve sub-microsecond latency, and natively integrate AI at every network layer.

> **The debate:** Some argue that 5G will be continuously updated through 3GPP releases (5G-Advanced = Rel 18/19) rather than 6G being a clean break — avoiding the massive investment of a brand-new system. History suggests there *will* be a 6G, but it will build heavily on 5G-Advanced foundations.

## Key Technologies Being Researched

### Terahertz (THz) Communications

- Frequencies: **0.1–10 THz** (above mmWave, below infrared)
- Enormous bandwidth available → potentially **Tbps-level data rates**
- Think of it as the next frontier after mmWave opened up spectrum above 24 GHz

**Challenges:**
- **Extreme atmospheric absorption** — water vapor absorbs THz waves heavily → very short range (meters to tens of meters)
- **Technology gap** — efficient THz transceivers don't yet exist at commercial scale
- **Path loss** — even worse than mmWave; inverse-square law at THz frequencies means rapid signal decay
- **NLOS impossible** — completely blocked by any obstacle

**Potential use cases:** Short-range ultra-high-speed links (data kiosks, device-to-device, backhaul for dense small cells, holographic communication)

> **Analogy:** If mmWave is like using a laser pointer (focused, doesn't go through walls), THz is like using UV light — even more focused, even more energy in a narrow beam, but absorbed by almost everything.

---

### Next-Gen MIMO

- 6G will extend Massive MIMO further — potentially **hundreds or thousands of antenna elements**
- At THz frequencies, wavelengths are sub-millimeter → antennas are **tiny** → can pack thousands onto a small panel
- **Holographic MIMO / Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS):** Smart surfaces that can dynamically shape the radio environment by reflecting/refracting signals — turning walls and buildings into "smart mirrors"

> **RIS Concept:** Imagine if the walls of a room could be programmed to reflect your WiFi signal exactly toward your device instead of scattering randomly. That's what RIS does at scale — it turns the propagation environment from a passive obstacle into an active participant.

---

### Dense Networks

- Even smaller cells than 5G small cells — potentially **attocells** (range: meters)
- Femtocells deployed at home/building level as standard infrastructure
- **Cell-free Massive MIMO** — no cell boundaries; distributed antennas cooperate to serve every user jointly

### New Waveforms

OFDM (used in 4G/5G) has served well but has inherent limitations for 6G requirements:

| Waveform | Full Name | Key Advantage | Key Challenge |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OFDM** | Orthogonal FDM (current) | Simple, well-understood | High PAPR, spectral leakage between subcarriers |
| **GFDM** | Generalized FDM | Flexible pulse shaping per subcarrier, lower OOB emissions | Higher complexity, self-interference between subcarriers |
| **FBMC** | Filter Bank Multi-Carrier | Excellent spectral containment (very low OOB), no CP needed | Long filter tails → higher latency, complex MIMO integration |
| **UFMC** | Universal Filtered Multi-Carrier | Filters groups of subcarriers (subbands), good balance | Moderate complexity, partial spectral containment |

> **Why move beyond OFDM?** OFDM wastes spectrum at band edges (guard bands) and power (high PAPR requires PA backoff). For 6G's extreme efficiency targets, new waveforms that are spectrally cleaner and power-efficient are needed. The likely outcome is **adaptive waveform switching** — different waveforms for different scenarios.

---

### New Modulation — APSK

| Modulation | Issue | Used In |
|---|---|---|
| PSK | High PAPR at high orders | Legacy systems |
| QAM | High PAPR; constellation points at varying distances from origin | 4G/5G (up to 256-QAM) |
| **APSK** | **Lower PAPR** — points arranged in concentric rings → more uniform power → PA can be more efficient | Satellite (DVB-S2), proposed for 6G |

> **Why PAPR matters:** High PAPR means the power amplifier (PA) must handle large peak-to-average power swings, forcing it to operate inefficiently (backed off from saturation). Lower PAPR → PA operates closer to saturation → less wasted power → longer battery life for UEs, lower electricity bills for base stations.

### Full Duplex (FD)

| Duplex Mode | How it Works | Used In |
|---|---|---|
| **FDD** | UL and DL on different **frequencies** | 4G/5G (paired spectrum) |
| **TDD** | UL and DL on same frequency but different **time slots** | 4G/5G (unpaired spectrum) |
| **Full Duplex** | Same frequency, **same time** for UL and DL simultaneously | 6G research |

**The challenge:** When a device transmits and receives simultaneously on the same frequency, its own transmitted signal (which is ~100 dB stronger than the received signal) overwhelms the receiver. This is called **self-interference**.

**Solutions being researched:**
- **Analog cancellation** — RF-domain suppression of the self-interference signal
- **Digital cancellation** — baseband processing to subtract the known self-interference
- **Antenna isolation** — physical separation and directional antennas
- Current lab demonstrations achieve ~110 dB of combined cancellation — just barely enough

**Benefit if achieved:** Theoretically **doubles spectral efficiency** — one frequency band does the work of two.

### Flexible Duplex

- UL/DL ratio can be adjusted **in real-time** based on instantaneous traffic demands
- Already partially in 5G NR (dynamic TDD) but 6G takes it further with per-slot, per-beam flexibility

## What 6G Might Enable

- **Holographic communication** — real-time 3D telepresence (requires Tbps bandwidth)
- **Tactile internet** — haptic feedback over wireless (requires sub-μs latency)
- **Digital twins** — real-time mirroring of physical world in virtual space
- **AI-native networking** — AI embedded at every layer (not added on top), self-optimizing
- **Complete IoT saturation** — every physical object connected and sensing
- **Joint communication and sensing (JCAS)** — network simultaneously communicates AND senses the environment (radar-like capabilities)

**2. Name three candidate waveforms for 6G and explain why OFDM may not be sufficient.**

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

Three candidates: **GFDM** (Generalized FDM), **FBMC** (Filter Bank Multi-Carrier), and **UFMC** (Universal Filtered Multi-Carrier).

OFDM limitations for 6G:
- **High PAPR** (Peak-to-Average Power Ratio) — forces power amplifiers to operate inefficiently
- **Spectral leakage** — energy spills between subcarriers and at band edges, requiring guard bands that waste spectrum
- 6G demands extreme energy efficiency (100× improvement over 5G) and spectral efficiency, which OFDM's inherent characteristics make difficult to achieve.

The likely 6G approach is **adaptive waveform switching** — using different waveforms for different scenarios.
</details>

---

**3. What is Full Duplex communication and why is it difficult to achieve?**

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**Full Duplex** means transmitting and receiving on the **same frequency at the same time** — unlike FDD (different frequencies) or TDD (different times). If achieved, it theoretically **doubles spectral efficiency**.

The fundamental challenge is **self-interference**: the device's own transmitted signal is approximately **100 dB stronger** than the distant received signal it's trying to hear. It's like trying to hear someone whisper while you're shouting through a megaphone.

Solutions being researched include analog cancellation (RF domain), digital cancellation (baseband), and antenna isolation. Current lab demonstrations achieve ~110 dB of combined cancellation, which is just barely sufficient for practical use.
</details>

---

**4. What is a Reconfigurable Intelligent Surface (RIS) and how could it help 6G?**

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

A **Reconfigurable Intelligent Surface (RIS)** is a flat surface covered with many small, programmable antenna-like elements that can dynamically **reflect, refract, or absorb** radio waves. Think of it as a "smart mirror" for wireless signals.

**How it helps 6G:**
- At THz/mmWave frequencies, signals are easily blocked by obstacles. RIS can be placed on walls, ceilings, and building facades to **redirect blocked signals** around corners and into shadowed areas.
- It turns the passive propagation environment into an **active, controllable** part of the network.
- Unlike a relay, RIS doesn't need to decode/re-encode signals — it simply reflects them with configurable phase shifts, making it **low-power and low-cost**.
- It enables coverage in scenarios where direct line-of-sight is impossible.
</details>
